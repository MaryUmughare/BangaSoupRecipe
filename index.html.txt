<h1>Banga Soup</h1>
<p>Banga Soup, also known as <em>Amuamiedi</em>, is a rich and nutritious Urhobo soup made from palm nut extract. It is a beloved staple across the Niger Delta region, best served with eba or starch.</p>
<h2>Why Banga Soup is Nutritious</h2>
<ul>
<li>Rich in minerals</li>
<li>Helps in the treatment of <strong>Vitamin A deficiency</strong></li>
<li>Rich in <strong>Vitamin K</strong></li>
<li>Rich in antioxidants</li>
<li>contains nutritients that support good brain functions</li>
</ul>

<!-- Ingredients -->
</h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul>
<li>1.5 lb Beef or any meat of your choice</li>
<li>1 large onion - half chopped, half-blended</li>
<li>1 teaspoon Cameroon Pepper (substitute with cayenne pepper)</li>
<li>2 teaspoons Seasoning powder or Bouillon cubes</li>
<li>3 medium Stockfish Panla (Okporoko) - soaked in hot water</li>
<li>1 can Palm-nut Extract</li>
<li>2 Scotch bonnets</li>
<li>1 tablespoon Banga Spice (Irugeje and Ataiko)</li>
<li>1-2 tablespoons Crushed Obeletientien leaves or Dried bitter leaves</li>
<li>1 Oburunbebe stick</li>
<li>2 medium Dried Fish - soaked and deboned</li>
<li>4 to 6 cups Water or Stock</li>
<li>1 tablespoon Ground Crayfish</li>
<li>Salt to taste</li>
</ul>
<!-- Cooking Instructions -->
<h2>Cooking Instructions</h2>
<ol>
<li>Boil the palm kernel fruit for 30 minutes until soft.</li>
<li>Pound gently in a mortar, strain through a sieve to extract liquid.</li>
<li>Boil the extract until it thickens and oil rises to the top.</li>
<li>Cook meat with onions, pepper, seasoning and salt until tender.</li>
<li>Add stockfish and cook for another 10 minutes, then set aside.</li>
<li>Add palm nut concentrate, dilute with stock, cook until oil floats.</li>
<li>Blend scotch bonnet and onion, add to pot.</li>
<li>Add Banga spice, fish, meat, crayfish and Oburunbebe stick. Cook 10 mins.</li>
<li>Stir in bitter leaves, simmer until thickened. Serve hot!</li>
<!-- Tips and Notes -->
<h2>Tips and Notes</h2>
<ul>
<li>Using canned palm nut extract? Skip steps 1 to 3.</li>
<li>Remove the <em>Oburunbebe stick</em> before serving.</li>
<li>Tastes even better the next day!</li>
</ul>
